Sarah's Story — Finding Myself Again

When my daughter was born, I felt like I should have been happier than I had ever been. I had everything I had always dreamed of — a beautiful baby, a loving partner and the life I thought I had been working towards.

But instead of feeling the overwhelming joy I expected, I felt empty, anxious and completely disconnected. I loved my baby deeply, but I struggled with feelings I couldn't explain. I felt exhausted, overwhelmed and like I was somehow failing at the one thing I was supposed to be naturally good at.

The hardest part was the shame. I was terrified that if I admitted how I felt, people would think I was ungrateful or a bad mum. So I kept smiling, telling everyone I was fine, while quietly feeling like I was falling apart.

Eventually, I realised I couldn't keep pretending. Opening up was one of the hardest things I have ever done, but telling my partner and allowing myself to accept support was the first step towards healing.

I learned that what I was experiencing was not a reflection of how much I loved my child, or the kind of person I was. Asking for help was not a weakness — it was an act of courage.

Today, I understand that even in the moments when we feel most lost, there is still a way forward. I no longer see that time as a failure. It was a chapter that taught me the importance of connection, honesty and showing myself the same kindness I give to others.
