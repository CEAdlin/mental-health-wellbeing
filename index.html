Sarah's Story — Finding Myself Again

When my daughter was born, I felt like I should have been happier than I had ever been. I had everything I had always dreamed of — a beautiful baby, a loving partner and the life I thought I had been working towards.

But instead of feeling the overwhelming joy I expected, I felt empty, anxious and completely disconnected. I loved my baby deeply, but I struggled with feelings I couldn't explain. I felt exhausted, overwhelmed and like I was somehow failing at the one thing I was supposed to be naturally good at.

The hardest part was the shame. I was terrified that if I admitted how I felt, people would think I was ungrateful or a bad mum. So I kept smiling, telling everyone I was fine, while quietly feeling like I was falling apart.

Eventually, I realised I couldn't keep pretending. Opening up was one of the hardest things I have ever done, but telling my partner and allowing myself to accept support was the first step towards healing.

I learned that what I was experiencing was not a reflection of how much I loved my child, or the kind of person I was. Asking for help was not a weakness — it was an act of courage.

Today, I understand that even in the moments when we feel most lost, there is still a way forward. I no longer see that time as a failure. It was a chapter that taught me the importance of connection, honesty and showing myself the same kindness I give to others.

James' Story — Finding Purpose Again

For years, I thought I was doing everything right. I followed the path I believed was expected of me — building a stable career, providing for my family and making choices that I thought would make the people around me proud.

But somewhere along the way, I lost sight of what I actually wanted. I had built a life based on what I thought I should do, rather than what gave me purpose or fulfilment.

Every day started to feel the same. Although everything looked fine from the outside, inside I felt disconnected, exhausted and like a part of me had gone quiet.

I realised I wasn't unhappy because I needed to completely change my life overnight. I was unhappy because I had stopped listening to myself and had forgotten what mattered to me.

With support, I started taking small steps to understand myself again. I made time for the things I had neglected, reconnected with old interests and began thinking about what I wanted my future to look like.

The biggest change was learning that progress didn't have to mean making huge, dramatic decisions. Small choices, made consistently, started to rebuild my confidence and helped me feel like myself again.

Today, I am still on that journey. I have a clearer sense of who I am and the kind of life I want to create. I am learning to make decisions based on my own values, rather than simply meeting the expectations of others.

There are still things I want to achieve and changes I want to make, but for the first time in a long time, I feel hopeful about the future.

I am no longer just going through the motions — I am creating a life that feels meaningful.

Anita's Story — Learning to Value Myself

For as long as I can remember, I struggled with feeling like I was never quite enough. Growing up in a difficult environment left me carrying beliefs about myself that I didn't realise were shaping the way I saw the world.

I became used to questioning my own worth and feeling like I always had something to prove. Over time, those feelings affected the choices I made, and I found myself settling for less than I truly deserved in my relationships and in the way I treated myself.

I often felt anxious, insecure and worried that I wasn't enough. Looking back, I can see that many of these patterns were connected to experiences from my past and the beliefs I had formed about who I was.

The turning point came when I started recognising these patterns and understanding where they came from. Through support networks, reflection and therapy, I began to challenge the stories I had carried about myself for so long.

Slowly, I started creating a new sense of identity — one that wasn't defined by difficult experiences from my childhood, but by my own values, strengths and the person I wanted to become.

The journey hasn't always been easy, but learning to value myself changed the way I approached everything. I became more confident in setting boundaries, expressing my needs and choosing relationships that felt healthy and supportive.

Today, I have built stronger connections with people who appreciate me for who I am. I continue to grow, learn and heal, but I no longer feel like I have to prove my worth.

I am creating relationships and a life that reflect the person I have always been underneath the fears and doubts.
